ABOUT PANG

Our mission

Pang is a message board for restaurants who wish to help hungry people in need. Restaurants that have leftover but safe-to-eat food can post Pang updates using a verified gmail account to inform the hungry of times they can pick up excess food.

This solution means that restaurants produce less waste, and fewer people go hungry. It’s a win-win situation! Pang does not profit off of these interactions, we merely seek to promote social good.



The USDA defines "food insecurity" as the lack of access, at times, to enough food for all household members. In 2011, households with children reported a significantly higher food insecurity rate than households without children: 20.6% vs. 12.2%.


Roughly 50 percent of all produce in the United States is thrown away—some 60 million tons (or $160 billion) worth of produce annually, an amount constituting “one third of all foodstuffs.”


The USDA has been working on a way to get business to reduce their amount of food loss by having 3 program: U.S. Food Loss and Waste 2030 Champions, Food Recovery Challenge and U.S Food Waste Challenge.


Huffington Post sources found that farmers themselves know that food is being discarded only due to the appearance. For instance, the article stated ‘ A cucumber farmer estimated that fewer than half the vegetables he grows actually leave his farm and that 75 percent of them removed before sale are edible.
